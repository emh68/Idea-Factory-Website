FAQs

General Information

Q:  What is The Idea Factory, and what kinds of projects can you do here?
A:  The Idea Factory is like a library, but instead of books, we have tools and equipment for building projects. For example, if you need a CNC machine for woodworking, audio equipment to start a podcast, or access to drones to learn how to fly them, our makerspace has you covered. A membership gives you access to our equipment like 3D printers, CNC machines, audio recording equipment, video studio with green screen, drones, and more to bring your ideas to life. We also offer courses to help you learn the skills to use our equipment and obtain employment. To see a list of our equipment (see Makerspace >> Equipment)

Classes and Programs

Q:  What classes are currently available, and who can enroll in them?
A:  Currently, we offer a Python Beginner Programming class and an Electronics Circuits Beginner class, both open to ages 12-16.
Q:  What skill levels are the classes aimed at?
A:  The classes are beginner-level, designed for those who are new to programming or electronics.
Q:  What is the class size limit, and can I join a waiting list if a class is full?
A:  Each class has a maximum of 10 students. If a class is full, you can join the waiting list to be notified about future openings.

Registration and Costs

Q:  How do I register for a class, and is there an age verification process?
A:  Registration is available on our website, and age verification is required for certain age-restricted classes.
Q:  What is the cost of a class, and are there any additional fees for materials?
A:  The initial registration fee is $100, part of the first month's $200 fee. Additional costs may apply for some materials, depending on the class.
Q:  Do you offer any discounts or scholarships for classes?
A:  Please contact us to learn more about potential discounts or scholarships that may be available.

Safety and Supervision

Q:  What safety guidelines are in place for kids and teens?
A:  We have strict safety guidelines, and staff are always present to supervise and assist kids and teens while they use equipment.
Q:  Do students need to bring their own protective gear, or is it provided?
A:  Protective gear is provided, but students are welcome to bring their own if they prefer.

Facilities and Environment

Q:  Are there restrooms and break areas available on-site?
A:  Yes, we have restrooms and break areas for users to take a break and enjoy refreshments.
Q:  Is there Wi-Fi available for use in the makerspace?
A:  Yes, we offer free Wi-Fi to all members in the makerspace.
